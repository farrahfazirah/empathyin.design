Biodiversity is declining at an alarming rate never seen before in human history.

There is nothing natural about losing nature. Our future depends on the decisions we make now.


Project Summary


At WWF-Singapore, we launched a web platform for people to voice their support for nature and the future they envision.

Timeline

Platform

Background

In conjunction with Earth Hour 2020, WWF-Singapore launched a digital campaign called 'an Open Letter to Singapore' to pledge support for environmental policies. The Open Letter provides Singaporeans a platform to be part of the movement to protect nature and biodiversity.

By adding their voice for nature, Singaporeans call for decision-makers in politics, business and education to improve environmental policies and business practices nationwide.

Why an Open Letter?

In 2019, WWF-Singapore successfully led the ban of ivory products through an open letter. Stakeholders wished to bring the open letter concept back.

Our target audience are citizens and residents of Singapore who care about Singapore's future.

Technical Requirements & Constraints

I gathered a list of technical requirements asked by stakeholders and pointed out the constraints for that particular requirement (if any).


Requirements Constraints
Users can share their letter on social media. NIL
Users can export their letter as a JPEG to share online. NIL
Mobile responsive. NIL
Users can preview the letter pre- and post- creation. Letter preview gets cut off in mobile breakpoint so users need to scroll down to see how their letter looks like.
Include grammar checker. We had to rule out grammar checkers as it is a paid service.
The letter is visible in preview displays on social apps. The letter may look cut off in the preview displays on social media. It is not possible to create 'view breakpoints' for static images.

Desired Outcomes

The open letter seeks three outcomes:

Maintain the good health of Singaporeans by addressing environmental threats caused by deforestation, exploitation and pollution.

Achieve a good quality of life for Singaporeans by preserving natural environments and biodiversity in Singapore and the region.

Ensure a bright and prosperous future for Singapore by ultimately stopping climate change.
