The Internet We Have Today is Broken.

Every time we interact over the Internet, copies of our data get sent to the server of a service provider, and every time that happens, we lose control over our data.

Background

About AlphaWallet
AlphaWallet is an open-sourced, non-custodial cryptocurrency wallet. We enable users to interact directly with blockchain-powered applications and services.

What's a Decentralised Browser?
A decentralised browser allows users to interact with decentralised applications (dapps) on the blockchain. The difference between the internet we all use today (centralised browsers) and decentralised browsers is in the backend.

Decentralised browsers run on a distributed peer-to-peer network — as opposed to typical applications running on centralised servers — which means users are accountable for their own data.

Problem

In August 2018, AlphaWallet implemented an in-app decentralised browser for fast and linear transactions on the blockchain. A month after implementation, user engagement rate stagnated indicating that the decentralised browser is not helping users to fulfil their crypto transactions.

Our Hypothesis

We believe that our existing browser lacks clear and effective call-to-actions to help users navigate the decentralised web.


Currently, the browser's homepage is linked to a blockchain directory site called "State of the Dapps".

We hypothesised that by designing clear call-to-actions on the homepage, it would help guide users to interact with decentralised applications on our browser and increase crypto transactions.

Insight
Users rely on mental model of browser systems

Our participants expected decentralised browsers to work the same way as the web and mobile browsers that we commonly use today.

We learned that our decentralised browser should conform to our users' existing mental model to ensure they have a smooth experience. New UX solutions should be introduced sparingly.

Insight
Competitors lacked customisation features for users

Users could not make any configurations to layout or content in our competitors' browser to meet their specific needs.

We strategised that empowering users with control to customise their browser experience could be a key differentiation strategy to outperform our competitors.

Feedback from the Blockchain Community

At AlphaWallet, we were fortunate to have a tight-knit community of blockchain geeks backing our mission and vision. We have a community of 500 people actively providing user feedback in our Telegram group.

I scoured through the Telegram group for their responses on their experience with dapps and dapp browsers.




Insight
Wants and Frustrations

Feedback from our research study and the community provided insight into what are their wants and frustrations when it comes to decentralised browsers.

Wants:

Users want to buy and sell tokens through the dapp browser so they don't have to constantly toggle between their wallet and dapp to check whether they've sent or received their token

Users want to use their tokens to interact with dapps


Frustrations:

Users find it hard to grasp the use of blockchain jargons such as 'ERC-721' and 'connected to local nodes'

Users are frustrated when a browser does not have an extensive dapp catalogue to make the decentralised web easy to explore

Users who are fairly new to the blockchain space don't know where to start on the dapp browser when there is a lack of call-to-action buttons
